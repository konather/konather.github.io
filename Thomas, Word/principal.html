Undergraduate Principal & Major

Private Lesson/Exam Information

 

 

Audition

Minimum performance level will be the student's ability to obtain membership in a performing brass ensemble and to meet the requirements on a per semester basis as outlined below

 

 

Lessons

Eight semesters of performance lessons are required

 

One 50-minute lesson per week

 

 

Ensemble Requirements

Participation in one of the Bands and/or Orchestra in addition to one of the following:
 

Trumpet Choir
 

Horn Choir
 

Trombone Choir

Tuba-Euphonium Choir

Brass Choir

Percussion Ensemble

 

 

Practice Requirements

10-14 hours weekly according to number of hours of credit for private lessons

 

Rehearsal time spent in official University groups will be given some consideration by the student's teacher toward fulfilling a small portion of the practice requirement. The majority of a student's practice hours should be spent practicing diligently on his assigned lesson material. Practicing for pep band or any other unofficial activity or group DOES NOT count toward fulfilling the practice requirement.

 

 

Lesson Materials

Students must provide their own methods books, sticks, and mallets

 

 

Recital Attendance

Attend at least five music recitals each semester. Not meeting this requirement will lower the lesson grade for the semester by one-third of a letter grade

Semester One

 

+A regular jury exam will be held at the end of semester one and will include the following:

 

 

+One secular solo (with score) with piano accompaniment

 

 

+The jury will issue exam grades, but the individual teacher will issue the final grade.  An average of the jury's grades will comprise one-third to one-half of the student's semester grade

 

+Technique (Please refer to Appendix A) in the keys of, F major/  d minor, B-flat major / g minor, and G major / e minor will be heard by two brass/percussion faculty during the last lesson of the semester.   All technique will be played in its entirety and will be played by memory.  Ranges of each technical requirement may be adjusted (by the teacher) to fit the abilities of each student.   Technique grades will be averaged with the jury's exam grades for the semester

 

+The student is encouraged to begin study with multiple tonguing (Please refer to Appendix C), sight-reading, ear training, clefs (Please refer to Appendix D) or transposition (Please refer to Appendix E), and lip trills

 

+By November, the student should select the piece he will perform in the Annual Music Contest in semester two

Semester Two

 

+The Freshman Advisory Test will be held at the end of semester two.  It will be 15 minutes in length and will include the following:

 

 

+The student must select two solos of contrasting styles and from different musical periods.  Both must have piano accompaniment and one must be memorized

 

 

+The evaluation of the Freshman Advisory Test will be as follows:  Encourage (grade of A - B);  Guarded Recommendation (grade of C); Discourage (grade of D); Strongly Discourage (grade of F).

 

 

+The jury will issue exam grades, but the individual teacher will issue the final grade.  An average of the jury's grades will comprise one-third to one-half of the student's semester grade

 

+Technique (Please refer to Appendix A) in the keys of C major / a minor, D major / b minor, and E-flat major / c minor will be heard by two brass/percussion faculty during the last lesson of the semester within the last week of classes.  All technique from this semester will be played in its entirety and will be played by memory.  Ranges of each technical requirement may be adjusted (by the teacher) to fit the abilities of each student.  A sampling of the technique from semester one will also be heard with a faculty member other than the student's teacher making the requests.  Technique grades will be averaged with the jury's exam grades for the semester

 

+The student is encouraged to continue study with multiple tonguing (Please refer to Appendix C), sight-reading, ear training, clefs (Please refer to Appendix D) or transposition (Please refer to Appendix E), and lip trills

 

+The student will finish the preparation of and perform one memorized secular solo in the Annual Music Contest

Semester Three

 

+A regular jury exam will be held at the end of semester three and will include the following:

 

 

+One secular solo (with score) with piano accompaniment

 

 

+The jury will issue exam grades, but the individual teacher will issue the final grade.  An average of the jury's grades will comprise one-third to one-half of the student's semester grade

 

+Technique (Please refer to Appendix A) in the keys of A-flat major / f minor, A major / f-sharp minor, and E major / c-sharp minor will be heard by two brass/percussion faculty during the last lesson of the semester.  All technique will be played in its entirety and will be played by memory.  Ranges of each technical requirement may be adjusted (by the teacher) to fit the abilities of each student. A sampling of the technique from semesters one and two will also be heard with a faculty member other than the student's teacher making the requests.  Technique grades will be averaged with the jury's exam grades for the semester

 

+The student is encouraged to continue study with multiple tonguing (Please refer to Appendix C), sight-reading, ear training, clefs (Please refer to Appendix D) or transposition (Please refer to Appendix E), and lip trills

 

+By November, the student should select the piece he will perform in the Annual Music Contest in semester four

Semester Four

 

+The Sophomore Platform Test will be held at the end of semester four. It will be given in two sections and will include the following:

 

A.

Two solos of contrasting styles and from different musical periods.  Both must have piano accompaniment and one must be memorized

 

 

These solos will be performed  publicly during a Performance Class within the last three weeks of the semester

 

B.

Held during the regular jury exam time

 

 

One etude,  not memorized,  chosen to  accurately demonstrate the performance and achievement level of the student

 

 

Sight-reading

 

 

Technique as outlined below for semester four

 

+This platform is a barrier exam which must be passed with a grade of  70%  or higher.  A failed Sophomore Platform Test will necessitate a major change by the student.

 

+The jury will issue exam grades, but the individual teacher will issue the final grade.   An average of the jury's grades will comprise one-third to one-half of the student's semester grade

 

+Technique (Please refer to Appendix A) in the keys of D-flat major / b-flat minor (C-sharp major / a-sharp minor),  B major / g-sharp minor (C-flat major /a-flat minor), and G-flat major / e-flat minor (F-sharp major / d-sharp minor) will be heard in the jury exam at the end of the semester.  All technique from this semester will be played in its entirety and will be played by memory.  Ranges of each technical requirement may be adjusted (by the teacher) to fit the abilities of each student.  A sampling of the technique from semester one, two, and three will be also be heard with a faculty member other than the student's teacher making the requests.  Technique grades will be averaged in with the jury's exam grades for the semester

 

+The student is encouraged to continue study with multiple tonguing (Please refer to Appendix C), sight-reading, ear training, clefs (Please refer to Appendix D) or transposition (Please refer to Appendix E), and lip trills

 

+The student will finish the preparation of and perform one memorized secular solo in the Annual Music Contest

Semester Five

 

+The student who passes the Sophomore Platform Test at the end of semester four will take the Junior Technique Test at the end of semester five.  This test is a barrier exam which must be passed with a grade of  80%  or higher before the Senior recital is presented.   Requirements are found in Appendices A, B, C, D, and E, with a descriptive summary of the test found in Appendix G.  With mutual consent of the student and teacher, this test may be taken ahead of schedule in the middle of the semester or in an earlier semester.  If such is the case, the student will have a regular jury exam at the end of semester five with the same requirements as semesters one and three.  There will be no required solo material on this test.

 

+The student is strongly encouraged to cover additional solo literature and perform in several brass performance classes throughout the semester

 

+The student is encouraged to continue study with multiple tonguing (Please refer to Appendix C), sight-reading, ear training, clefs (Please refer to Appendix D) or transposition (Please refer to Appendix E), lip trills, and additional scale studies with increased speeds (Please refer to Appendix F).

 

+By November, the student should select the piece he will perform in the Annual Music Contest in semester six

Semester Six

 

+A regular jury exam will be held at the end of semester six, unless the student is scheduled for a Senior Recital Note Check, and will include the following:

 

 

+One secular solo (with score) with piano accompaniment

 

 

+The jury will issue exam grades, but the individual teacher will issue the final grade.  An average of the jury's grades will comprise one-third to one-half of the student's semester grade

 

+A Senior Recital Note Check will replace the jury exam in semester six if the student's Senior Recital is scheduled during semester seven.   Please refer to the literature requirements listed for the Senior Recital Note Check as found in semester seven

 

+The student is strongly encouraged to cover additional solo literature and perform in several performance classes throughout the semester

 

+Technique covered during this semester (Please refer to Appendix F), in the keys assigned by the student's private teacher, will be heard by two brass faculty during the last lesson of the semester during the last week of classes.  All technique will be played by memory.  Ranges of each technical requirement may be adjusted (by the teacher) to fit the abilities of each student.  Technique grades will be averaged with the jury's exam grades for the semester

 

+The student is encouraged to continue study with multiple tonguing (Please refer to Appendix C), sight-reading, ear training, clefs (Please refer to Appendix D) or transposition (Please refer to Appendix E), lip trills, and additional scale studies with increased speeds (Please refer to Appendix F)

 

+The student will finish the preparation of and perform one memorized secular solo in the Annual Music Contest

Semester Seven

 

+Senior Recital Scheduling

 

 

+Music education students may NOT present their recital during the semester in which they do their student teaching

 

 

+Before the Senior Recital can be presented, the student must have passed Advanced Harmony and Advanced Aural Skills

 

+The student will choose literature for his Senior Recital according to the following guidelines

 

 

+Church Music principal - 19-23 minutes.  The literature must include works from at least two major style periods and at least one sacred solo or arrangement

 

 

+Music Education principal - 19-23 minutes.  The literature must include works from at least two major style periods

 

 

+Performance major - 40-50 minutes.  The literature must include works from at least three major style periods

 

+The Senior Recital Note Check will be held at the end of semester seven if the student's  Senior  Recital is scheduled during semester eight

 

 

+The entire recital must be performed with piano accompaniment

 

 

+Any concertos included on the recital must be performed from memory.

 

 

+If a section of the memory piece(s) is not memorized, the check will be failed.   The pieces do not need to be at performance tempo, but must show accurate preparation, continuity, and, where applicable, memory

 

 

+If a re-take is necessary, the entire program must be repeated

 

+The Senior Recital Check will be scheduled four weeks before the actual date of the Senior Recital.   The recital should be performance ready for this check.  Sunday dress is appropriate
