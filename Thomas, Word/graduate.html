Graduate

Private Lesson/Exam Information

 

Audition

The graduate audition for a principal must include selections which represent three major style periods.  These selections should be on a difficulty level of IV-V on a scale of I-VI.

 

The graduate audition for a performance major must include selections which represent three major style periods.  No memory is required for the audition. The majority of these selections should be on a difficulty level of V on a scale of I-VI.

Lessons

Four semesters of performance lessons are required

 

 One 50-minute lesson per week

 

 

 

Ensemble Requirements

 Participation in one of the Bands and Orchestra in addition to one of the following:

 

 

Trumpet Choir

 

 

Horn Choir

 

 

Trombone Choir

 

 

Tuba-Euphonium Choir

 

 

Brass Choir

 

 

Percussion Ensemble

 

 

Practice Requirements

15-25 hours weekly according to number of hours of credit for private lessons

 

Rehearsal time spent in official University groups will be given some consideration by the student's teacher toward fulfilling a small portion of the practice requirement. The majority of a student's practice hours should be spent practicing diligently on his assigned lesson material. Practicing for an unofficial activity or group DOES NOT count toward fulfilling the practice requirement.

 

 

Recital Attendance

Attend at least five music recitals each semester. Not meeting this requirement will lower the lesson grade for the semester by one-third of a letter grade

Semesters One and Two

 

+A regular jury exam will be held at the end of semester one and two and will include the following:

 

 

+One secular solo (with score) with piano accompaniment

 

 

+The jury will issue exam grades, but the individual teacher will issue the final grade.  An average of the jury's grades will comprise one-third to one-half of the student's semester grade

 

Semesters Three & Four

 

+Graduate Recital

 

 

+Literature should reflect some research for literature of high quality which transcends the mundane, everyday teaching materials

 

 

+Performance Majors - at least three style periods must be included.  It is suggested that a chamber work also be included in the recital.  An encore is optional.

 

 

+Church Music principals - several sacred solos or arrangements must be included on the program in addition to works from two or three style periods.

 

 

+Any concertos included on the recital must be performed from memory

 

+A Graduate Recital Note Check will replace the jury exam in semester.

 

 

+Every solo piece to be played on the recital must be included on the note check

 

 

+Pieces do not need to be at performance tempo but must show continuity

 

 

+Any concertos included on the recital must be performed from memory.  Pieces to be performed from memory must be completely memorized.

 

+The Graduate Recital Check will be scheduled four weeks before the actual recital date. The recital should be performance ready for this check.
